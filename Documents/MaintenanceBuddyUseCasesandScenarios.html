Maintenance Buddy Use Cases and Scenarios

Team members:

Ashton Hull, Jordan Glatz, Daniel Vincent

1. User Registration

When first downloading and opening the app, users will first register an account using an email address and password. The app checks whether an account already exists with that email before creating a new one, and if not, the system will then create their account and securely save their information. After successfully registering, the user is moved to the main dashboard, where they can begin adding vehicles and tracking maintenance history.

2. Adding a Vehicle Profile to Dashboard

Once the account is created, the user will then be sent to their dashboard, where they can add their first vehicle. Users will do this by clicking the add vehicle option and then entering the year, make, model, current mileage, fuel type (petrol, diesel, hybrid, or electric), and yearly mileage estimate. Based on the selected fuel type, the system presents a default maintenance schedule. Users can customize this schedule by adjusting each service's mileage interval, removing services, or opting to skip the first reminder for services that have already been recently performed. This completes the process to add a vehicle profile to your dashboard. With it added, users can click on the vehicle profile in order to view information regarding that vehicle. This vehicle profile is also where users will go in order to add service events and upload photos.

3. Creating Service Events

Within the user's vehicle profile, they can begin adding service events. To do this, there will be a button within the profile that lets users add their specific event. After pressing, the app then prompts the users for the specific details of their event. These details include the date of the event, the mileage of the vehicle at the time, the service type, mechanic shop, and a description. Users may also select tags such as "I did this service myself" (DIY), "Cosmetic modification", or "Performance modification". Additionally, users can attach up to 3 photos (PNG/JPEG) and 1 document (PDF/TXT) to the service event, each limited to 10MB. After adding their event, it is then saved onto the vehicle timeline that the user can view at any time within their profile. Following this, the user will then get confirmation from the system that the event was successfully added.

4. Appointment Reminders

When users schedule maintenance for their vehicle with a mechanic, they may create a service entry. Instead of logging the service right away, they may choose to record the appointment first. The user opens their vehicle profile and selects the option to add an appointment. They then will be prompted to enter the date and time of the appointment. Users may also, in addition to this, enter the name of the mechanic shop who is doing work on the vehicle. Also, they can optionally enter the work that is being done. The app will then save the appointment to the vehicle's timeline as a service event. Furthermore, the app will send notifications for other events automatically based on vehicle usage and mileage. These reminders will be generated by comparing the vehicle’s maintenance schedule with the time or mileage since the last logged service.

5. Uploading documents and Photos

Users may upload supporting photos and files when creating a service event. When creating a service event, users enter details such as date and mileage. Users may then attach up to 3 photos (PNG/JPEG) and 1 document (PDF/TXT). When attaching a photo, users will be prompted to either take a photo using their camera or choose an existing photo from their storage. All attachments are limited to 10MB each. The system will verify the size and type of the file in order to make sure it is valid. If the file is not valid for any reason, the app will prevent the upload and display a corresponding error message.

6. Exporting Maintenance Summary

Users can export their maintenance history at any time from the reports screen. There will be two forms the user can choose from: a maintenance summary or a full report. Users may export a summary for a single vehicle or for all vehicles combined. When users choose the option to export as a maintenance summary, it will be exported as a PDF containing the most recent 30 service entries. If users choose to export as a full report, the system will generate a ZIP archive containing the complete maintenance history for that vehicle, including a summary file, vehicle data, service records, and all attachments organized in a dedicated folder. Once the export is ready, the user can download or share the file using the device's native share sheet. A risk to this may be insufficient storage space or a failed download. If this is the case, the app will display an error message and prompt the user to try again.

7. Editing and Deleting Service Events

Users can choose any previously created service event and examine the maintenance schedule for their car. They will be able to alter or remove the event by selecting it. The date, mileage, service description, and any attached files can all be changed by users. The system will save the changed data and update the vehicle timeline after modifications are performed. Before permanently deleting the service event, the system will ask the user for confirmation. Reports, reminders and the vehicle history timeline will no longer display deleted service occurrences.

8. Updating Vehicle Mileage

Users can update the mileage of their car at any time. The user may choose the update mileage option after opening their vehicle profile. The current odometer reading for the car is then entered. Future maintenance reminders are recalculated by the system based on the comparison between this figure and the manufacturer maintenance schedule. This procedure maintains the accuracy of alerts while lowering estimating mistakes. The system verifies that the modified mileage has been saved.

9. Managing Multiple Vehicles

Within their accounts, users are able to manage multiple vehicles. Users can see a list of every car connected to their profile from the dashboard. They can add new service events or see any vehicle's maintenance history. Additionally, users have the option to modify or remove vehicle profiles as needed. All related service events and paperwork will be deleted from the system along with the vehicle. This guarantees that users may keep distinct, well organized logs for every vehicle

10. Viewing Maintenance History Timeline

The whole maintenance history of their car can be viewed by users on a timeline within their vehicle profile in a chronological format. This timeline will show service events as well as appointment entries. To see all the information, including miles, service description, pictures, and papers, users can enlarge any event. Until they are marked as finished, appointment entries will remain on the timeline and become service events. This tool makes it simple for customers to monitor overall vehicle care and review previous maintenance

11. Notification Center

The app includes a dedicated notification center accessible from the main tab bar. Notifications are automatically generated by the system based on two criteria: when a vehicle's estimated mileage is approaching the interval for a required service, and when an appointment is scheduled for the following day. Within the notification center, notifications are separated into "Unread" and "Earlier" sections. Users can tap a notification to navigate directly to the related vehicle's profile. Users can mark individual notifications as read or use the "Mark All as Read" option. An unread count badge is displayed on the notifications tab so users can see at a glance if they have pending alerts.

12. Managing Required Services

Users can view and manage the required services for any of their vehicles on a dedicated screen accessible from the vehicle profile. Each required service displays its name, the mileage interval at which it is due, and the mileage at which it was last performed. Users can edit the mileage interval for any service, with a minimum interval of 1,000 miles. Users can also enable or disable individual services. Disabled services will not generate maintenance reminders. This allows users to fine-tune their maintenance schedule after the initial vehicle setup as their driving habits or vehicle needs change.

13. User Settings and Preferences

The app provides a settings screen where users can manage their account and preferences. Users can change their password by requesting a password reset email. Users can toggle notifications on or off to control whether they receive maintenance and appointment reminders. Users can also switch between imperial (miles) and metric (kilometers) units, which affects how mileage is displayed throughout the app. Finally, users can sign out of their account from the settings screen.
